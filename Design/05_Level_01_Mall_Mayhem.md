# Cat Ninja 3 — Level 01 Design Brief

## Level Name
Mall Mayhem

## Alternate Working Titles
- Atrium Ambush
- Retail Rampage
- Cat in the Mall

## Level Role
This is the MVP vertical slice level. It must teach the core movement and combat systems, establish tone and world identity, and culminate in the laser T-Rex boss fight.

## Level Fantasy
The player is a ninja house cat moving through an exaggerated urban-commercial interior that feels like a mall seen through the scale and imagination of a real cat. Everyday fixtures become traversal routes, combat spaces, and dramatic action set pieces.

## Design Goals
- Establish the game’s urban-domestic visual identity
- Teach movement through level layout, not text alone
- Teach ammo use and ranged precision
- Introduce grannies as readable absurd-comedy enemies
- Introduce catfood, shuriken pickups, and catnip
- Deliver one satisfying boss encounter
- Prove the full loop of traversal, combat, resource use, checkpointing, and victory

---

## Environment Language
The level should evoke:
- polished tile floors
- kiosks
- benches
- planters
- storefronts
- signage
- escalators
- balcony edges
- food-court-like open spaces
- service corridors
- maintenance access spaces
- atrium architecture

Everything should feel plausible within a mall, but exaggerated into action-platforming scale for a cat protagonist.

---

## Core Traversal Themes
- long horizontal routes broken by vertical climbs
- platforming across fixtures and mall structures
- wall-jump shafts and signage routes
- high/low path variation
- readable arena pockets for combat

---

## Level Flow Overview

### Section 1 — Entry / Service Access
Purpose:
- Introduce basic movement
- Establish scale and tone
- Ease the player into navigation

Features:
- simple jumps
- safe surfaces
- one or two visual gags
- first mall interior reveal

### Section 2 — Public Concourse Traversal
Purpose:
- Introduce wider movement space
- Teach double jump and wall jump by layout

Features:
- benches, planters, kiosk roofs
- elevated platforms
- optional small pickup route

### Section 3 — First Enemy Encounters
Purpose:
- Introduce combat granny types
- Teach spacing and punish windows

Features:
- one melee granny encounter
- one ranged granny encounter
- simple arena geometry
- first catfood pickup nearby

### Section 4 — Precision Route / Shuriken Teaching
Purpose:
- Teach the utility of mouse-aimed ranged attacks
- Begin ammo-management awareness

Features:
- enemy on higher platform
- breakable or targetable object
- shuriken pickup placed to reinforce intended use
- slow-mo aim opportunity

### Section 5 — Catnip Segment
Purpose:
- Introduce frenzy mode as a tactical powerup
- Give player a short empowerment sequence

Features:
- catnip pickup
- safe moment to understand manual activation
- short combat/traversal section where frenzy feels useful

### Section 6 — Pre-Boss Transition
Purpose:
- Raise tension
- Create boss reveal anticipation
- Place checkpoint

Features:
- visual reveal of atrium
- audio shift or environmental emphasis
- last resource placements
- checkpoint before boss arena

### Section 7 — Boss Arena: Mall Atrium
Purpose:
- Deliver level climax
- Test movement, combat, ammo economy, and super use

Features:
- open floor
- layered platforms
- clear sightlines
- laser T-Rex boss
- victory on defeat

---

## Enemy Placement Philosophy
- early enemy count should stay low
- first encounters should isolate enemy behaviors
- later encounters may combine melee + ranged granny pressure
- enemies should be placed to reward movement and timing, not crowd spam

---

## Pickup Placement Philosophy

### Catfood
- placed after early mistakes are likely
- used to support learning, not trivialize the level

### Shuriken Pickups
- placed before or after ranged challenge moments
- enough to support active use
- not so frequent that ammo feels irrelevant

### Catnip
- placed deliberately where manual activation timing matters
- should support decision-making, not automatic chaos

---

## Checkpoints
Recommended checkpoint count for MVP:
- 1 mid-level checkpoint
- 1 pre-boss checkpoint

If the level is kept short, a single pre-boss checkpoint may be enough.

---

## Boss Arena Brief

### Arena Identity
A mall atrium with multiple visual layers and enough open space for laser sweeps and repositioning.

### Arena Features
- central open floor
- side platforms or balcony ledges
- strong visibility
- limited clutter
- room for jump and wall interaction if needed

### Boss Goals
The boss should:
- feel absurd and memorable
- test movement timing
- test shuriken use
- create one strong heroic finish to the level

---

## Visual Style Notes
- modern HD-2D-inspired presentation
- pixel-art character sprites
- layered mall background planes
- selective glow, light, and depth
- readable silhouettes over clutter

---

## Success Criteria
This level succeeds if:
- the player understands the core fantasy quickly
- movement feels good by the first few minutes
- the ranged/melee/powerup loop feels cohesive
- the level theme feels distinct and memorable
- the T-Rex boss provides a satisfying climax
